# Cyber Policy, Privacy & Incident Governance

## Overview

This repository consolidates cybersecurity law, policy, privacy, and governance analysis into a recruiter-facing portfolio artifact. It focuses on how organizations interpret security obligations, respond to incidents, handle privacy risks, and align security programs with legal and regulatory expectations.

## Objectives

- analyze privacy and security obligations across modern technology environments
- connect law and policy to practical incident governance
- interpret regulatory expectations such as reporting, safeguards, and accountability
- demonstrate structured written communication around security governance issues

## Project Highlights

- Examined IoT cybersecurity and privacy challenges across legal and operational dimensions
- Analyzed NIST SP 800-53 as a control framework rather than a simple checklist
- Compared FTC and CFAA approaches to cybersecurity accountability
- Reviewed reporting and regulatory themes such as CIRCIA
- Explored ransomware and critical infrastructure implications through the Colonial Pipeline case
- Incorporated privacy, search/seizure, and governance-oriented reasoning

## Topic Areas

- IoT privacy and security regulation
- incident reporting and governance
- legal accountability in cybersecurity
- control frameworks and policy interpretation
- privacy, data handling, and regulatory alignment
